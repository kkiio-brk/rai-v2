Rural Access Index (RAI)

This is a measure of the proportion of the rural population living within 2 kilometres of an all-weather road. The RAI is important as it measures people's transport accessibility in rural areas.

To determine where people live, or the population distribution in rural areas. During calculation of Rural Access Index Kenya National Bureau of Statistics(KNBS) Population Data is used.
This data is interpolated to generate a continous surface representing population per 100 square kilometers.

The roads data comes from Road Inventory Condition (RICS) survey which is carried out every five years by Kenya Roads Board. This data is used for planning of road maintenance. The survey assess the type of surface and the condition of approximately 420,000 road segments, covering almost 250,000 kilometres.

Since the Rural Access Index measures road access for rural populations only, urban areas need to be excluded from the calculation. To separate urban from rural areas, Urban Areas Classifciation data from MINISTRY OF TRANSPORT, INFRASTRUCTURE, HOUSING, URBAN DEVELOPMENT AND PUBLIC WORKS is used.

The Rural Access Index is calculated at the county level in Kenya.
